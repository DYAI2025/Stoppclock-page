# Breathing: The Calm Anchor

> Your breath is the remote control for your nervous system.

## Character

The Breathing Timer is not about time passing, but about time deepening. It is a visual guide to sync your internal rhythm with a calming external pace. It transforms the screen from a source of distraction into a point of focus and tranquility.

## Rituals

### The Morning Box

Before opening emails, complete four cycles of Box Breathing (4-4-4-4). Use this square of time to build a mental foundation for the day.

### The Evening Release

Use the 4-7-8 rhythm to signal your body that the work day is over. With each long exhale, imagine tension leaving your shoulders.

### The Panic Reset

If stress spikes, focus entirely on the expanding and contracting circle. Let your breath follow the animation without force. Three cycles are often enough to regain perspective.

## Facts

### Box Breathing & Navy SEALs

This technique (Inhale 4, Hold 4, Exhale 4, Hold 4) is famously used by Navy SEALs to remain calm and focused in high-stress combat situations.

### The 10,000 Breaths

We take about 20,000 breaths a day. Most are unconscious and shallow. Conscious breathing is one of the few autonomic functions we can directly control.

### Dr. Weil's 4-7-8

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil, the 4-7-8 pattern (Inhale 4, Hold 7, Exhale 8) is described as a "natural tranquilizer for the nervous system".
