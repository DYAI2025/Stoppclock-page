Quotes, Sayings, and Fun Facts (English)

The Saying: "Time's up" (Die Zeit ist abgelaufen) 






Origin: This phrase comes directly from the courtrooms of ancient Greece and Rome. Water clocks (Klepsydra) were used there to limit the speaking time of the parties. The emptying of the water container signaled the end of the allotted time.






The Expression: "o'clock" 


Origin: The English expression "o'clock" is a shortening of "of the clock". It was used to distinguish the new, mechanically measured time from the natural time (the position of the sun).


The First Alarm Clock (c. 325 BC) 






Inventor: Plato 






Function: The philosopher constructed a water clock in which a vessel slowly filled, then tipped , causing lead balls to fall noisily onto a copper plate. This signaled the beginning of class to his students.





The Alternative Alarm Concept


Inventor: Leonardo da Vinci 



Function: He devised a more drastic waking method: a machine that pulled the blanket off the sleeper.



The Cinematic Countdown (1929) 





Inventor: Fritz Lang 





Origin: The countdown was not invented by NASA , but by the German director Fritz Lang for his silent film "Frau im Mond" (Woman in the Moon). He used the backward count as a purely dramatic device to build suspense before the fictional rocket launch. Rocket engineers and NASA later adopted the idea for real launches.






The "Pomodoro" Technique (1980s) 






Inventor: Francesco Cirillo 






Origin: The time management method is named after a tomato-shaped kitchen timer (Italian: pomodoro) that the inventor used as a student to structure his study sessions.





Rome's 100-Year Time Error 






Fact: When the Romans captured a sundial from Sicily during the First Punic War and set it up in Rome , they failed to consider that a sundial must be calibrated for its specific longitude (or latitude). The result was that the clock stubbornly showed the wrong time in Rome for over 100 years.






The "Happy Time" (10:10) 





Fact: Watches in display windows and advertisements are often set to 10:10. The reason is purely psychological: the symmetrical position of the hands resembles a smile and is intended to evoke positive emotions in the buyer.






Big Ben's Fine-Tuning 




Fact: The famous clock in London is regulated using old penny coins. Placing a coin on the pendulum adjusts the speed by 0.4 seconds per day.






Spain's "Social Jet Lag" 




Fact: Spain geographically lives in the wrong time zone. Dictator General Franco aligned the clocks with Nazi Germany in 1940 as a gesture of solidarity. This decision was never reversed.






The Backward-Running Clock 






Location: La Paz, Bolivia 






Fact: The clock on the congress building has been running backward since 2014. This is a political symbol of decolonization and a return to the identity of the southern hemisphere.






China's Single Time Zone 


Fact: Although China geographically spans five time zones , the entire country uses only one official time: Beijing Time. This means that in the far west of the country, the sun doesn't rise until around 10 a.m. in the summer.




The Most Time Zones 


Fact: Not Russia or the US, but France holds the record for the most time zones with 12. This is due to its overseas territories scattered across the globe.


The Apollo 13 Rescue 


Fact: After the electronics and digital onboard clocks failed on the Apollo 13 mission , the crew used their mechanical Omega Speedmaster chronographs. They used them to time a critical 14-second engine burn that was essential for their safe return to Earth.






The "Rolex-Killer" 




Fact: A murderer (Albert Johnson Walker) was identified because his victim's (Ronald Platt) Rolex watch stopped after his death. Based on the remaining power reserve and the serial number , the time of death could be narrowed down , leading investigators to the killer.






Smelling the Time 


Fact: In China, there were incense stick clocks. A specially shaped incense stick burned down slowly, and different sections were scented with different fragrances. You could literally smell the time.
